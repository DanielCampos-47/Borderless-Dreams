What Exactly Is Financial Aid?

Financial aid is funding attained through grants, work-study, loans, or scholarships designated to help you pay for college or career school. Keep reading to learn about financial aid opportunities open to the undocumented community and what applications to stay away from based on your unique status.

The Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)

What is FAFSA? A form that students use to apply for federal financial aid. Undocumented students, including DACA recipients, do not qualify for federal student aid. The office of Federal Student Aid, the largest provider of student financial aid in the US, make college a possibility through FAFSA and other methods.

Is FAFSA undocumented friendly? FAFSA is not available to undocumented students because a SSN is required to create a FederalStudentAid ID, parents do not require a SSN. Many non-U.S. citizens qualify for federal student aid, refer to the FSA(FederalStudentAid) wesbite to determine your elegibility.

FAFSA alternatives Undocumented students can apply for the NYSDA(New York State Dream Act).

Financial aid tied to FAFSA + other federal aid Federal Pell Grants only for undergraduate students, FSEOG(Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant), TEACH(Teacher Educational Assistance for College and Higher Education) Grant, Iraq and Afghanistan Service Grant, Federal Work-Study Program, Federal Student Loans, Federal Aid for International Study, education tax benefits, community service AmeriCorp awards, ETV(Educational and Training Vouchers) for Young People Currently and Formerly in Foster Care, and scholarship or loan repayment programs through Indian Health Service, National Institutes of Health, and National Health Service Corps.

The Senator Jose Peralta NYS DREAM Act

What's the NYS DREAM Act? Undocumented students can apply for the NYSDA(New York State Dream Act) to become eligible for New York State-administered student financial aid such as TAP(Tuition Assistance Program), the Excelsior Scholarship, the Enhanced Tuition Awards program, the STEM Incentive Program and more. If you qualify for state aid under the NYS Dream act, you must separately qualify and apply for each financial aid program separately!

Is it safe to apply? Families don't have to fear deportation, colleges are prohibited by law from reporting a student's immigration status without student consent. New York State and your college are bound by law to keep your immigration status confidential. The information you provide will only be used to determine eligibility for awards and to adiminister financial aid you are able to receive.

The New York State Tuition Assistance Program (TAP)

What's TAP? TAP(New York State Tuition Assitance Program) can award an annual grant of up to $5,665, grants do not have to be paid back. TAP is available in New York State approved schools in CUNY, SUNY, and any non-for-profit independent degree-granting college on a part-time basis. Part-time TAP is available for part-time students, given they fulfill some prerequisites.

Applying for TAP through the NYS DREAM Act 1) Students applying for NYS financial aid for the first time must first apply for eligibility throught the NYS DREAM act.
2) If a student is declared eligible under the NYS DREAM act, to apply for TAP they simply need to start a new application.
3) After submitting your application it is up to you to monitor its status online, expect an email determining your eligibility.
4) If you are offered an award, the last step is to accept the award through your colleges financial aid portal.

The Excelsior Scholarship Program

What's the Excelsior Scholarship? After other federal and state grants and scholarships have been applied, the Excelsior Scholarship then will cover any remaning gap in tuition expenses up to $5,500. It is only available for New York residents who attend an undergraduate program at a SUNY or CUNY. If you are in an undergraduate program of study, the scholarship will support you for up to five years given that you complete a total of 30 credits each academic year. If you are in an opportunity program, the scholarship will support you for up to five years given that you complete a total of 24 credits each academic year.

Why Should You Pay Taxes?

If you wish to work towards U.S. citizenship, having a good moral character is essential for the success of your applications. Paying taxes as an undocumented person is the perfect way to demonstrate that you want to be here; nothing says "I want to be a U.S. citizen" more than supporting tax-funded public assistance programs that you don't benefit from just because of your status.
