What Exactly Is Financial Aid?

Financial aid is funding attained through grants, work-study, loans, or scholarships designated to help you pay for college or career school. Keep reading to learn about financial aid opportunities open to the undocumented community and what applications to stay away from based on your unique status.

The Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)

What is FAFSA? A form that students use to apply for federal financial aid. Undocumented students, including DACA recipients, do not qualify for federal student aid. The office of Federal Student Aid, the largest provider of student financial aid in the US, make college a possibility through FAFSA and other methods.

Is FAFSA Undocumented Friendly? FAFSA is not available to undocumented students, a SSN is required to create a FederalStudentAid ID. Many non-U.S. citizens qualify for federal student aid, refer to the FSA(FederalStudentAid) wesbite to determine your elegibility.

FAFSA Alternatives

Financial Aid Tied to FAFSA + Other Federal Aid Federal Pell Grants for undergraduate students, FSEOG(Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant), TEACH(Teacher Educational Assistance for College and Higher Education) Grant, Iraq and Afghanistan Service Grant, Federal Work-Study Program, Federal Student Loans, Federal Aid for International Study, education tax benefits, community service AmeriCorp awards, ETV(Educational and Training Vouchers) for Young People Currently and Formerly in Foster Care, and scholarship or loan repayment programs through Indian Health Service, National Institutes of Health, and National Health Service Corps.
