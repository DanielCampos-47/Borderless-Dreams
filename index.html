About This Site

This wesbite was developed to demistify the postsecondary process for the undocumented student population in New York City. The goal is to mold it into a stepping stool to help undocumented people across the states reach their career goals.

Common Questions

What Is a Grant?  ▼

Funds provide by a public body, charitable foundation, or a specialised grant-making institution to an indiviaul or another entity for a specific purpouse linked to public benefit. Unlike a loan, a grant does not have to be paid back.

What Is a Loan?  ▼

A sum of money that is borrowed and expected to be paid back with interest.

What Is an Undocumented Student?  ▼

A foreign national that has entered the United States without inspection or with fraudulent documents.
Or entered legally as a non-immigrant but then violated the terms of their status and remained in the United States without authorization.

What is FAFSA?  ▼

A form that families normally use to apply for financial aid. The aid from FAFSA comes from the federal government, not allowing undocumented students from qualifying.

What Resoures Are Barred Behind FAFSA?  ▼

The PELL Grant, open only to undergraduate students.

What Is the DREAM Act?  ▼

The Senator Jose Peralta New York State DREAM Act gives undocumented/ other students access to New York State-administered grants and scholarships that cover higher education cost. Though not recommended, it also opens up the ability to take out a loan.

What Resources Does the DREAM Act Unlock?  ▼

TAP, a need based New York State tuition grant that can cover up to $5,165.

The Excelsior Scolarship Program, a tuition grant for SUNY and CUNY which covers up to $5,500.

The Enhanced Tuition Award Program, a tuition award for private college attendance which can cover up to $6,000.

Do I Have to Pay Back Financial Aid?  ▼

Grants and scholarships unlocked by the NYS DREAM Act will NOT have to be repaid, given all of their unique requisites to receive funds are met.

Is Applying to College or Trade School Dangerous?  ▼

The NYS Dream Act application and any college you choose to share your immigration status with are bound by law to keep that information confidential.
